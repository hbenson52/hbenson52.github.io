dfOpiod['MedianHouseholdIncome'] = (dfOpiod['MedianHouseholdIncome'] / 1000)

##determine Income/Opiod relationship and defineplot type, x/y axis values, size, and color of dots
##I chose a scatterplot to best determine the relationship between income and opiod overdoses. It easily shows how one relates to the other.
ax = dfOpiod.plot(kind='scatter', x='MedianHouseholdIncome', y='FPOO-Rate', figsize=(8,5), color='darkblue')

##set labels for x & y axes and title
ax.set_xlabel('Median Household Income', fontsize=12)
ax.set_ylabel('Number of Opiod Overdoses', fontsize=12)
ax.set_title('Opiod Overdoses Relative to Income ($Thousands) per 100k residents', fontsize=14)
plt.show()
  1. Do opioid overdoes tend to be associated with less affluent areasthat is, areas where families have lower incomes?
    • According to the data and how it presents itself within the scatterplot, it shows opiod overdoses are associated with less affluent areas. The trend of the plotted data points show the bulk of the of overdoses happen when income is between 30 and 80 thousand with fewer overdoses when income is between 80 and 120 thousand.
